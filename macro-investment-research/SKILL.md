## Step 1 — Frame the Research Question

Clarify before researching:
1. **Asset type**: Public equity / private company / commodity / macro theme / sector?
2. **Time horizon**: Trading idea (< 1 year) / investment (1–5 years) / structural theme (5+ years)?
3. **Purpose**: New idea → thesis build | Existing position → update/pressure test | Deal context → sector backdrop?
4. **Output destination**: Notion page / Word memo / PPTX / conversation?

---

## Step 2 — Research Framework

### For a Company or Investment Thesis

**Business quality**
- What does the company do and how does it make money?
- Competitive moat: pricing power, switching costs, network effects, cost advantage?
- Management quality and alignment
- Capital allocation track record

**Financial quality**
- Revenue growth, margin trajectory, FCF conversion
- Balance sheet: net debt/EBITDA, covenant headroom
- ROIC vs. WACC trend

**Valuation**
- Current EV/EBITDA, P/FCF vs. history and peers
- Implied growth rate at current price — is it reasonable?
- Downside scenario: what's the multiple on trough earnings?

**Catalyst / setup**
- What changes the price from here? (earnings inflection, re-rating, macro tailwind, deal)
- What's the timeline?

**Risks**
- What kills the thesis? (key man, commodity price, regulation, leverage)
- What would make you sell?

### For a Macro Theme or Sector

**Supply / demand dynamics**
- Where are we in the cycle? (early, mid, late, trough, peak)
- Supply constraints or gluts?
- Demand drivers and durability

**Pricing outlook**
- Spot vs. forward curve
- Historical range and mean-reversion tendency
- CAD/USD sensitivity (important for Canadian industrial companies)

**Equity implications**
- Which stocks/sectors benefit most?
- Lead indicators to watch
- Positioning: consensus vs. contrarian?

## Step 3 — Commodities & Macro (Paul's Industry Context)

When research touches scrap metal, steel, or recycling:
- **Key commodities**: HMS #1 (ferrous benchmark), LME copper, LME aluminum, shredded scrap
- **Drivers**: Auto production (non-ferrous), construction (ferrous), China steel output, USD strength
- **CAD/USD**: Critical — scrap prices are USD-denominated; CAD weakness = margin tailwind for Canadian recyclers
- **Seasonality**: Q1 typically soft (winter), Q2/Q3 peak collection season
- **Electric arc furnace (EAF) growth**: Structural tailwind for scrap demand as EAF share of steel production increases globally

## Web Research Guidelines

Use web search for:
- Current commodity prices and forward curves
- Recent earnings / guidance from relevant companies
- Macro data: CPI, PMI, housing starts, auto production
- M&A activity in relevant sectors
- Analyst reports or industry reports (summarize; don't reproduce)

Always note the date of data pulled. Flag if key data is stale.
