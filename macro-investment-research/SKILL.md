## Thesis Structure
Every thesis output follows this structure:

**One-line thesis:** The bet in plain English.

**Setup:** Why does this opportunity exist now? What is the market missing or mispricing?

**Catalysts:** Specific events or data points that move the thesis. (3-5 items, dated where possible.)

**Variant view:** What does consensus believe, and why is Paul's view different?

**Risks:** What kills the thesis? (3-5 items, honest.)

**Position / structure:** How to express the view (equity, debt, private asset, options).

**Timeline:** When to expect thesis to play out.

**Exit:** What price, multiple, or event triggers a sale.

## Sector Research Template

**Sector / Geography:**

**Supply-demand fundamentals:**
- Current supply (capacity, utilization, new builds)
- Demand drivers (who buys, why, how much)
- Price dynamics (spot vs. contract, trend)

**Competitive landscape:**
- Market structure (fragmented / consolidated / oligopoly)
- Key players (public comps, private operators)
- Barriers to entry

**Regulatory environment:**
- Canadian-specific: NEB, AER, provincial regs, carbon pricing
- Tailwinds / headwinds from policy

**SK/AB private deal implications:**
- Sectors with elevated deal activity
- Distressed or oversupplied areas
- Operator quality in region

**Public market expression:**
- TSX/TSX-V listed comps
- US-listed with Canada exposure
- ETFs / indices
